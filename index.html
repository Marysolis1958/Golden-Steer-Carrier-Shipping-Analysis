Details
Tins are manufactured in China and shipped via ocean freight to a U.S. port, then trucked to Goldleaf's warehouse in Birmingham, AL. Stored at $20 per pallet per month. This step drives the total lead time and cannot be expedited.
Phase 02 — Parallel
Label Sourcing
Goldleaf · AL → Denver, CO
Labels Ordered
↔ Parallel — No Added Lead Time
Details
Labels are ordered from Goldleaf before the PO is placed and shipped directly to RMSC in Denver, CO. This step runs simultaneously with Phase 01 — it does not extend the total lead time.
Phase 03
Blending & Production
Rocky Mountain Spice Co. · Denver, CO
Raw Spices Sourced
Formula Blended
Tins Filled, Sealed & Labeled
Details
Tins arrive from Alabama. RMSC sources raw spices, blends the formula, fills the tins, seals them, and applies labels. Note: Production is scheduled 4 weeks out — the first 4 weeks involve ingredient sourcing and setup before blending begins.

Inbound Logistics
Freight · Denver, CO → Las Vegas, NV
Finished Goods Freighted CO → NV
Details
Finished goods are freight-shipped from Denver, CO to Golden Steer's warehouse in Las Vegas, NV. Transit takes less than one week.

Order Fulfillment
Golden Steer · Las Vegas, NV
Orders Picked & Packed
Shipped to Customer
Details
Golden Steer receives orders, picks tins from the warehouse shelf, packs them, and ships directly to the customer within 1–2 business days.
Executive Takeaway — To be in stock by September (Labor Day), packaging procurement must begin no later than April. Tin sourcing from China via ocean freight is the critical path — this lead time cannot be expedited. Labels run in parallel and do not add to the total timeline.

🔒 Approval Gate 1 of 2

Wizard Pins provides pricing, PDF proof, and sizing for approval. The Golden Steer team must review and approve the PDF before the physical proof begins. Duration depends on team alignment speed — this is the most common source of delays.

Steak — Weekly Market Cadence
Days 1–5Market Monitoring (Weekly)
Day 5–6PO Placed — Meat Cut
Day 6–7Delivery to Warehouse

~9 Days TotalMarket-Driven Cadence
Phase 01
Market Price Monitoring
Golden Steer & Outwest · Las Vegas, NV
Weekly Price Trend Published by Outwest
Fri–Sun: Pricing Set for Following Week
↻ Recurring Weekly Decision
Details
Outwest publishes market price updates Friday–Sunday for the following week. If prices are stable: order as-needed for the next two weeks. If prices are trending up: complete demand forecasting and place a bulk cut order to lock in cost.
Phase 02
PO Placed — Meat Cut
Outwest Warehouse · Las Vegas, NV
Meat Cut to Order at Outwest
Stored at Outwest Freezer
★ Invoiced on Pull — Not at Cut
Details
Meat is cut on a weekly basis. A minimum of 2 cases per active SKU are kept at Outwest's freezer. For bulk orders, Outwest stores all cases. Golden Steer is only invoiced when inventory is pulled — not at the time of the cut.
Phase 03
Delivery to Warehouse
Outwest → Golden Steer Warehouse · Las Vegas
Cases Delivered to GS Warehouse
Held in GS Freezer
Details
Golden Steer places a delivery PO and meat cases are delivered from Outwest's warehouse to Golden Steer's Las Vegas warehouse, where they are held in the freezer until ordered by a customer.
Phase 04
Order Fulfillment
Golden Steer · Las Vegas, NV
Steak Picked from Freezer
Packed & Shipped to Customer
Details
Golden Steer receives orders, picks the steak from the freezer shelf, packs it with appropriate cold packaging, and ships it out to the customer within 1–2 business days.
Executive Takeaway — This is a weekly, market-driven supply chain — not a fixed lead time. Golden Steer monitors Outwest pricing every Friday–Sunday to decide the following week's strategy. Stable prices: order as-needed (2-week rolling). Rising prices: demand forecast and bulk cut to lock in cost. Outwest invoices Golden Steer only when inventory is pulled — a meaningful cash flow advantage.
